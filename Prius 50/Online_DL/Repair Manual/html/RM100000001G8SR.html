Title: EXTERIOR PANELS / TRIM: NAME PLATE: REMOVAL; 2019 - 2022 MY Prius [12/2018 -        ]

REMOVAL

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

HINT:

When removing the name plates, heat the vehicle body, back door outside garnish and name plates using a heat light.

Heating Temperature

Item

Temperature

Vehicle Body

40 to 60°C (104 to 140°F)
  • Back Door Outside Garnish
  • Name Plate

20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F)

CAUTION:

  • Do not touch the heat light and heated parts, touching the heat light may result in burns.
  • Touching heated parts for a long time may result in burns.

*a

Heated Part

*b

Heat Light

NOTICE:

Do not heat the vehicle body, back door outside garnish or name plate excessively.

PROCEDURE

1. REMOVE NO. 2 LUGGAGE COMPARTMENT DOOR NAME PLATE

(a) Apply protective tape around the No. 2 luggage compartment door name plate as shown in the illustration.

Protective Tape

(b) Using a moulding remover, separate the double-side tape and disengage the 2 pins to remove the No. 2 luggage compartment door name plate.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the back door outside garnish.

2. REMOVE NO. 1 BACK DOOR NAME PLATE

(a) Apply protective tape around the No. 1 back door name plate as shown in the illustration.

Protective Tape

(b) Using a moulding remover, separate the double-side tape and disengage the 2 pins to remove the No. 1 back door name plate.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the back door outside garnish.

3. REMOVE NO. 4 LUGGAGE COMPARTMENT DOOR PLATE

(a) Apply protective tape around the No. 4 luggage compartment door plate as shown in the illustration.

*a

Wooden Block

*b

Piano Wire

Protective Tape

(b) Insert a piano wire between the back door outside garnish and No. 4 luggage compartment door plate.

(c) Tie objects that can serve as handles (for example, wooden blocks) to both wire ends.

(d) Pull the piano wire and cut the double-sided tape that holds the No. 4 luggage compartment door plate to the back door outside garnish to remove the No. 4 luggage compartment door plate.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the back door outside garnish.

4. REMOVE BACK DOOR ORNAMENT SUB-ASSEMBLY

(a) Apply protective tape around the back door ornament sub-assembly as shown in the illustration.

Protective Tape

(b) Using a moulding remover, separate the double-side tape and disengage the 2 pins to remove the back door ornament sub-assembly.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the back door outside garnish.

5. REMOVE SIDE PANEL EMBLEM

HINT:

Use the same procedure for the RH side and LH side.

(a) Apply protective tape around the side panel emblem as shown in the illustration.

*a

Wooden Block

*b

Piano Wire

Protective Tape

(b) Insert a piano wire between the vehicle body and side panel emblem.

(c) Tie objects that can serve as handles (for example, wooden blocks) to both wire ends.

(d) Pull the piano wire and cut the double-sided tape that holds the side panel emblem to the vehicle body to remove the side panel emblem.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the vehicle body.
