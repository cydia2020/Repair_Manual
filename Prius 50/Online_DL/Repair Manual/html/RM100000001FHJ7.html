DESCRIPTION

The airbag ECU assembly has a built-in yaw rate and acceleration sensor and detects the vehicle condition using 2 circuits (GL1, GL2).

The skid control ECU (brake booster with master cylinder assembly) receives signals from the hybrid vehicle control ECU, steering angle sensor, certification ECU (smart key ECU assembly), inverter with converter assembly, yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly), ECM and main body ECU (multiplex network body ECU) via CAN communication.

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

U0073 and U0074:

  • When communication by the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) or steering angle sensor takes longer than a certain time when in the bus off state (number of sending errors exceeds a certain value) or the load on the bus is high, the skid control ECU (brake booster with master cylinder assembly) judges that there is a CAN communication error and illuminates the MIL and stores a DTC.

U0100 (Case 1 and 2), U0110 (Case 1 and 2), U0125 (Case 1 and 2), U0140, U0293 (Case 1 and 2) and U1107:

  • When a communication error with any ECU continues for a certain amount of time or communication is unstable (communication is invalid for a certain amount of time a certain amount of times), the skid control ECU (brake booster with master cylinder assembly) judges that communication with the respective ECU is abnormal and illuminates the MIL and stores a DTC.

CONFIRMATION DRIVING PATTERN

  1. Connect the Techstream to the DLC3.
  2. Turn the power switch on (IG).
  3. Turn the Techstream on.
  4. Clear the DTCs (even if no DTCs are stored, perform the clear DTC procedure).
  5. Turn the power switch off.
  6. Turn the power switch on (IG).
  7. Turn the Techstream on.
  8. Wait 60 seconds.
  9. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRAC / Trouble Codes.
  10. Read the DTCs.

    HINT:

    • If a DTC is output, the system is malfunctioning.
    • If a DTC is not output, perform the following procedure.
  11. If the DTCs are not output, perform a universal trip and check for permanent DTCs.

    Click here

    HINT:

    • If a permanent DTC is output, the system is malfunctioning.
    • If no permanent DTCs are output, the system is normal.
