INSTALLATION

PROCEDURE

1. INSTALL NO. 4 ANTENNA CORD SUB-ASSEMBLY

(a) When reusing the No. 4 antenna cord sub-assembly:

(1) Engage the claw as shown in the illustration to temporarily install a new No. 1 wire harness protector.

Install in this Direction

HINT:

Use the same procedure for the No. 2 wire harness protector.

(2) Install the No. 1 wire harness protector to the grommet as shown in the illustration.

HINT:

Use the same procedure for the No. 2 wire harness protector.

Install in this Direction

(3) for Back Door Side:

  1. Connect the rubber protrusion of the grommet to the No. 2 wire harness protector with tape.
  2. Connect the antenna cord to the No. 2 wire harness protector with tape (w/o Rear Wiper).
  3. Connect the antenna cord and washer hose to the No. 2 wire harness protector with tape (w/ Rear Wiper).

(b) Engage the 8 claws to connect the 2 grommets.

(c) w/o Rear Wiper:

(1) Connect the connector.

(2) Engage the 2 clamps.

(3) Connect the connector to install the No. 4 antenna cord sub-assembly.

(d) w/ Rear Wiper:

(1) Engage the clamp.

(2) Connect the washer hose.

(3) Connect the connector.

(4) Engage the 3 clamps.

(5) Connect the washer hose.

(6) Connect the connector to install the No. 4 antenna cord sub-assembly.

5. INSTALL NO. 2 ANTENNA CORD SUB-ASSEMBLY

HINT:

Butyl tape and adhesive tape are not available as supply parts. If these pieces of tape still have enough adhesion to secure the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly to the roof headlining assembly, reuse them. If the adhesive tape and/or the butyl tape is no longer sticky, apply new tape following the procedure below.

(a) Apply new butyl tape.

(c) Install the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly to the roof headlining assembly from the marking tape (A) to the adjustment area.

NOTICE:

  • Make sure that there are no gaps between the roof headlining assembly and No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly, and that the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly is not twisted.
  • Make sure the No. 2 antenna cord assembly is securely installed. If any part of the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly is loose, it will cause an abnormal noise.

(d) Apply the adhesive tape (A) as shown in the illustration.

(e) Engage the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly to the notch of the roof headlining assembly.

(f) Align the marking tape (B) on the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly with the marking on the roof headlining assembly and secure the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly with the adhesive tape (B) as shown in the illustration.

(g) Install the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly to the roof headlining assembly from the marking tape (B) to the adjustment area.

NOTICE:

  • Make sure that there are no gaps between the roof headlining assembly and No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly, and that the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly is not twisted.
  • Make sure the No. 2 antenna cord assembly is securely installed. If any part of the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly is loose, it will cause an abnormal noise.

HINT:

Secure the extra length of the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly in the adjustment area.

(h) Apply the adhesive tape (C) as shown in the illustration to secure the No. 2 antenna cord sub-assembly.
